Our editorial standard is **useful before persuasive**. Every guide should help a reader make a more informed decision even if they do not purchase anything.

## How topics are selected

We prioritize common, specific decisions where clearer criteria can reduce confusion: choosing a format, comparing practical features, understanding routine fit, or recognizing an important tradeoff. Search interest may help us find a question, but it does not determine our answer.

## Research and sources

Depending on the topic, our review may include:

- Current manufacturer instructions and ingredient or specification pages
- Retailer listings used to confirm availability, variants, and packaging
- Guidance from relevant government agencies and established professional organizations
- Published research when a health-related claim requires context
- Repeated patterns in verified-owner feedback, treated as observations rather than proof
- Hands-on use, only when the article explicitly says that testing occurred

We distinguish direct observations, manufacturer claims, and editorial inference. A product is never described as personally tested unless it was actually used by the reviewer.

## How recommendations are made

We define the intended reader and criteria before selecting products. Criteria may include routine fit, ease of use, maintenance, packaging, scent, size, replaceable parts, current availability, and total cost of ownership. The weight of each criterion changes with the use case.

We do not award placement because a brand offers a higher commission, sends a sample, or requests coverage. Sponsored content, if introduced in the future, will be clearly labeled and kept distinct from independent recommendations.

## Affiliate relationships

BPC Hub may earn a commission when a reader purchases through a labeled affiliate link. Affiliate eligibility and commission rates do not determine the editorial conclusion. See our [Affiliate Disclosure](/affiliate-disclosure/) for details.

## Health-related content

Our content is general education, not individualized medical advice. Health-related claims receive additional source review and a visible disclaimer. We avoid diagnosing readers and direct symptoms, adverse reactions, or individual treatment questions to qualified healthcare professionals.

## Automation and editorial review

Software may assist with organizing research, checking links, formatting drafts, or identifying pages that may need updates. Automated output is not treated as a source. An editor is responsible for checking the page, its claims, disclosures, and links before publication.

## Updates and corrections

We update a page when a material specification, formula, product status, source, or recommendation changes. The “Updated” date reflects a substantive review, not a cosmetic edit.

If we find an error that could change a reader’s decision, we correct it promptly and update the page’s substantive review date when appropriate. A public correction channel will be added when the dedicated domain mailbox is activated.
